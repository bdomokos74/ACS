The Art of Computational Science

A series of books on how to build a computational lab

© 2003- Piet Hut and Jun Makino
The aim of our series is to provide a student with a hands-on guide to building a computational laboratory, and doing state-of-the-art research with it. The series will be self-contained: a high-school student should be able to start at page 1, and work her way through the series. [more . . .]

The central aim of our book series is to break down the barrier between research and education. From an educational point of view, our approach is unusual in that we aim to lead a student along the shortest path from an elementary introduction to the cutting edge of research. At the same time, the reverse is true as well: from a research point of view, we want to illustrate how extensive documentation is beneficial for a research project. [more . . .]

We have coined the term Variational Programming to describe our prefered approach to programming: to follow a path of least action. The name is borrow from physics, where Hamilton's variational principle states that a physical trajectory of a particle is given by a path along which the action is minimal. [more . . .]

Our main goal in this series is to propose a new approach to both research and education in computational science, bringing out all aspects: letting the science, the arts and the crafts aspects of research all be present in the educational offering. We expect that here will be others who share our enthusiasm, after seeing what we have begun. [more . . .]
